# Vault

This repository is a personal Obsidian vault for capturing work reflections, lessons, friction points, ideas, and open questions over time.

## Purpose

This vault is meant to help:

- document what happened and what was learned
- capture friction before it is forgotten
- preserve ideas that may be worth revisiting later
- record open questions that can guide future exploration
- create a history that can be analyzed for trends over time
- Identify recurring themes, strategic insights, and opportunities for action

## How To Use This Vault With AI Tools

This vault is designed to be both a place to write notes and a place to review them later with AI tools.

### Basic Workflow

1. Add notes to the `logs/` folder using the template in `templates/`.
2. Keep entries short, honest, and specific.
3. When enough notes have accumulated, open the vault with an AI tool and ask it to review the notes for patterns, signals, themes, or open questions.

### Practical Instructions

Use an AI tool to look across the vault and help synthesize what is emerging.

Examples:

- "Review the vault and tell me what themes are emerging."
- "Summarize the main ideas across my recent notes."
- "What recurring friction shows up in these entries?"
- "Pull out the biggest open questions from the last few notes."
- "What signals are starting to repeat across multiple entries?"
- "Turn the last two weeks of notes into a short summary."
- "What tensions or contradictions show up in my thinking?"
- "What opportunities seem underexplored based on these notes?"
- "What principles or beliefs seem to be forming across these entries?"
- "Group my notes into themes and give each one a short label."

### Best Practices

- Ask focused questions when you want sharper synthesis.
- Ask broader questions when you want unexpected patterns to surface.
- Revisit older notes periodically to compare how your thinking is evolving.
- Use AI tools to summarize, cluster, and reflect, but keep the raw notes lightweight and easy to maintain.

### Guiding Idea

The goal of the vault is not just to store notes. The goal is to build enough useful signal over time that AI tools can help turn those notes into insight.

## Structure

- `logs/`: dated daily or weekly reflection notes
- `templates/`: reusable note templates, including the daily log structure
- `.obsidian/`: local Obsidian configuration for the vault

## Note Format

Most entries follow a simple structure:

- `Learned`
- `Friction`
- `Ideas`
- `Questions`

This format is designed to make notes quick to write and easier to synthesize later.

## How This May Evolve

As the vault grows, it may become useful to:

- summarize notes by week, month, project, or theme
- identify recurring pain points or opportunities
- track how thinking changes over time
- connect insights across teams, meetings, and initiatives
- turn raw notes into actions, recommendations, or strategy
