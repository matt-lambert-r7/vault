Week of April 20-24	
### Learned:
- More about roles of different members on the design team; who does what
- Limitations around how R7 platform is currently setup
- Design team is high skilled, works well together, supportive and helpful
- I'm noticing a belief on the team that power users are ok with more complexity, in their face, all at once.
- Alerts and investigations are not 1:1 in R7
- The Unified Platform design and ux work is solid
### Friction
- The team is large, it can be hard at times to coordinate and see all the moving parts
- Investigations is an area that has not had alot of investment
- Analyst, customer, and non-AI users present 3 different personas, with different goals, and different UIs which makes more complex UX situations
- I tend to believe even power users should have a simple experience, usually guided through progressive disclosure
- The UI is in transition from a legacy system to a new and improved system. It can be confusing to understand what is current and which component patterns should be used. 
- Can be hard to trace ownership across teams
### Ideas
- Investigations should be rebuilt from the ground up leaning more heavily on progressive disclosure.
- Look for areas to focus beyond 90 day delivery plan

### Questions
- Can we unify all the investigation views into a single view, then toggle controls on/off depending on role?
- Can we use progressive disclosure more with power users to make their experience simpler?
- Can we apply the unified platform design more broadly to start to paint a picture of how it would look applied more widely?
- What areas can be explored beyond the 90 delivery plan?
