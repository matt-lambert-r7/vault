- I'm getting a better handle on the different user personas but still need to focus here more to learn the differences in UX/UI

### Friction
- Scope can balloon quickly as we give more power to users
- There are valid arguments to provide guardrails or not provide guardrails
- Guardrails help to direct the user, no guardrails allow us to better measure the types of questions users have
- Headless solution requires RBAC and other safety controls
- Source is an entity that is not as commonly displayed in Rapid7 UI. eg: Datadog
- Closing alerts in R7 vs closing an investigation in kenzo, needs resolution and one way forward

### Ideas
- Implement Kenzo chatbot as is
- Consider a headless option that allows the user to plug Codex, Claude Code, other AI tools directly into their instance of the platform
- Headless tool can also integrate with other services like google calendar. Cross referening where a user should be based on their calendar with what we are seeing in alerts could help investigations
- I want to be able to execute actions of all types inside the chatbot and headless experiences
- I want to be able to work with raw data inside the chatbot and headless experiences
- We may need a dedicated Alert Cluster/Type view, likely a tab with detail page showing which alerts are in the cluster
- There may be an opportunity to explore how Detection rules work on the R7 Platform. Unclear if they exist today. Detection rules can be written with AI assistance
- Do a deeper review of R7 vs Kenzo alerts detail and identify any differences in the experiences
- Instead of forcing a user to learn the platform, we could give them the chat UI... just let them ask whatever question they want which may get them to an answer quicker. If they do not find what they need, the chatbot can still point them to the right place in the UI to investigate more manually
- Allow users to generate visualizations on data in the chat UI... eg: Generate me a bar chart of alerts for this user for the last 14 days
